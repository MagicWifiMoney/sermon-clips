# Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) Analysis for Sermon Clips

## Primary ICPs

### 1. Church Communications Director
**Job Title Variations:**
- Communications Director
- Director of Communications
- Church Communications Manager
- Digital Communications Director

**Responsibilities:**
- Oversee live broadcasts and recordings of church services
- Upload sermons to online platforms (YouTube, website, etc.)
- Manage church announcements across all channels
- Oversee media volunteers
- Ensure service runs without technical difficulties
- Advertise church events through announcements
- Manage all church online platforms and social media
- Work closely with pastoral staff to communicate messages

**Qualifications:**
- Typically requires 4-year bachelor's degree in communications or digital communications
- Relevant experience in media/communications
- Technical skills in live streaming and recording platforms
- Biblical leadership abilities
- Strong communication skills

**Pain Points:**
- Responsible for entire communication ecosystem (overwhelming scope)
- Managing volunteers with varying skill levels
- Preventing technical difficulties during live services
- Keeping up with announcements across multiple ministries
- Balancing live streaming, recording, and post-production
- Limited time to create engaging social content from sermons
- Need to reach both in-person and online congregations

### 2. Social Media Manager (Church)
**Job Title Variations:**
- Social Media Manager
- Social Media & Content Manager
- Social Media Specialist
- Digital Outreach Coordinator

**Responsibilities:**
- Produce, manage, and analyze church's social media presence
- Create content for multiple platforms (Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, YouTube)
- Collaborate with Creative and Pastoral Teams
- Align content with church messages, promotions, and goals
- Coordinate social media and digital outreach strategy
- Engage congregation and reach new audiences

**Reports To:**
- Senior Pastor
- Communications Director
- Creative Director

**Pain Points:**
- Weekly clips, captions, thumbnails across multiple platforms (IG, FB, TikTok, YouTube)
- Avoiding burnout from constant content creation
- Making content when "nothing going on worth posting about"
- Not enough photos or videos available
- All-volunteer teams (no professional resources)
- Managing multiple platforms with different format requirements
- Creating engaging content that doesn't feel too promotional

### 3. Media Director
**Job Title Variations:**
- Media Director
- Director of Media
- Media & Production Director
- Creative Director

**Responsibilities:**
- Lead media teams for church services and events
- Maintain high-level production value
- Serve as primary Front of House engineer
- Audio operation and technical direction of all programs
- Train and supervise media team members
- Lead overall media strategy
- Create all digital and physical media under tight deadlines

**Reports To:**
- Lead Pastor
- Executive Pastor

**Pain Points:**
- Responsible for creation of ALL digital and physical media
- Working under tight deadlines
- Training and supervising volunteers
- Maintaining production quality across all services/events
- Balancing live production with post-production needs

### 4. Senior/Lead Pastor
**Responsibilities (Related to Media):**
- Deliver weekly sermons
- Approve messaging and communications
- Oversee church growth strategy
- Coordinate with communications team
- Ensure message reaches beyond Sunday service

**Pain Points:**
- Spends hours preparing sermons, but impact limited to Sunday
- Message forgotten by Tuesday
- Wants to reach broader audience beyond physical attendees
- Limited time to personally manage social media
- Needs team to extend sermon impact throughout week
- Concerned about church growth and community reach

### 5. Executive/Associate Pastor
**Responsibilities:**
- Oversee multiple ministry areas
- Coordinate between pastoral and communications teams
- Manage church operations
- Support lead pastor's vision

**Pain Points:**
- Coordinating between multiple departments
- Ensuring consistent messaging across all channels
- Limited budget for professional services
- Volunteer management challenges

## Secondary ICPs

### 6. Worship/Creative Pastor
- Oversees creative elements of services
- May manage media/communications in smaller churches
- Concerned with visual presentation and engagement

### 7. Youth/Student Pastor
- Needs engaging content for younger demographics
- Heavy focus on social media (TikTok, Instagram)
- Limited resources for professional content creation

### 8. Church Administrator
- Manages church operations and budget
- Evaluates ROI of communications tools
- Seeks cost-effective solutions for multiple needs

## Church Size Segments

### Small Churches (Under 200 attendees)
- Often one person wearing multiple hats
- All-volunteer teams
- Very limited budget
- Need simple, affordable solutions
- May be pastor + one volunteer

### Mid-Size Churches (200-500 attendees)
- May have part-time communications staff
- Mix of staff and volunteers
- Moderate budget constraints
- Need scalable solutions

### Large Churches (500-2000 attendees)
- Dedicated communications team
- May have multiple staff members
- Better budget but higher expectations
- Need professional-quality outputs

### Mega Churches (2000+ attendees)
- Full communications department
- Professional staff
- Larger budgets
- May have in-house editors but still need efficiency tools
- Multi-campus considerations

## Decision-Making Process

**Primary Decision Makers:**
- Senior/Lead Pastor (final approval, especially in small churches)
- Communications Director (primary user and advocate)
- Church Administrator (budget approval)

**Influencers:**
- Media Director
- Social Media Manager
- Executive Pastor
- Volunteer team members (if they love it, they'll advocate)

**Budget Authority:**
- Small churches: Senior Pastor
- Mid-size: Executive Pastor or Administrator
- Large: Communications Director with pastoral approval
- Mega: Department budget holder

## Common Buying Triggers

1. **Burnout Prevention**: "How can you possibly keep this schedule up without it controlling your life?"
2. **Volunteer Turnover**: Losing key media volunteers
3. **Growth Plateaus**: Need to expand reach beyond physical attendance
4. **Algorithm Changes**: Social media reach declining
5. **New Pastor/Staff**: Fresh perspective on communications
6. **Budget Availability**: End of fiscal year, new budget cycle
7. **Competitive Pressure**: Seeing other churches with better social presence
8. **Event Promotion**: Big upcoming event requiring social push
